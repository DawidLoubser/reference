Welcome to the world of XHTML

(The gentleman's HTML)

XHTML documents, like this one, enable a standard method of communicating across the web. Without standards like these, the web cannot continue to evolve in a controlled manner. The story thus far goes:

  1. First there was HTML 1.0, a relatively pure language invented by Tim Berners-Lee to exchange information.
  2. Then, marketing people got hold of the internet, and pages erupted into "tag soup", manifested in its most extreme form in the impure HTML version 4.0.
  3. XHTML attempts to eradicate the mess of the 1990s, and bring balance to the online universe. But the road is long...
