This site represents a resource for the collaborators in the URDAD-MDE project which has received funding within the Technology and Human Resources for Industry Programme (THRIP) of the National Research Foundation of South Africa. The URDAD-MDE research group is part of the Software Science and Formal Methods research group led by Dr. Stefan Gruner of the Computer Science, University of Pretoria.

Scope of project

The project is in the domain of model driven engineering. Its scope, as per the proposal to the NRF, is to

  • complete the formalization of UML including the formalization of
    • the URDAD model structure,
    • proving model qualities like completeness, consistency, testability and traceability,
    • the definition IEEE 1471 compliant URDAD views,
    • the verifiability of an URDAD model through an OCL based validation suite, and
    • to compare the URDAD methodoloy with other formal and semi-formal approaches.
  • assess the viability of using model-driven engineering and MDA technologies like QVT in particular to perform perform model transformation tasks including
    • implementation mapping onto widely used reference architectures like Java EE and SOA,
    • documentation generation including UML-to-English mapping,
    • test generation from URDAD contracts including functional and quality testing.
